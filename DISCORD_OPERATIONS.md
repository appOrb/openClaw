# Discord Operations Guide

How to use Discord channels for effective company operations.

---

## Your Daily Workflow

### Morning (9:00 AM UTC)
1. Check **#owner-dashboard** for daily summary
2. Review **#daily-standup** for full team status
3. Check **#incidents-escalations** for any overnight issues
4. Scan **#company-announcements** for updates

**Time:** 5-10 minutes

### Midday Check
1. Monitor **#owner-dashboard** for real-time updates
2. Check **#project-status** for active work progress
3. Review any @mentions in **#incidents-escalations**

**Time:** 2-3 minutes

### Evening (5:00 PM UTC)
1. Check **#owner-dashboard** for metrics summary
2. Review **#metrics-reports** for detailed KPIs
3. Check **#financial-ops** for budget/spending updates

**Time:** 5-10 minutes

### Weekly (Sunday 8:00 PM UTC)
1. Review weekly summary in **#owner-dashboard**
2. Plan next week priorities
3. Post strategic direction to **#executive-suite**

**Time:** 15-20 minutes

## Channel-by-Channel Usage

### MONITORING Category (Your Command Center)

#### #owner-dashboard
**Purpose:** Your single source of truth  
**Check:** Multiple times daily  
**What you'll see:**
- Daily standup summaries (9 AM)
- Metrics summaries (5 PM)
- Weekly summaries (Sunday 8 PM)
- Critical escalations
- Approval requests

**Actions:**
- Respond to escalations within SLA
- Approve/deny major decisions
- Ask clarifying questions

#### #metrics-reports
**Purpose:** Detailed KPI tracking  
**Check:** Daily at 5 PM or when making decisions  
**What you'll see:**
- Revenue metrics (MRR, customers, churn)
- Agent operations (status, activity, success rate)
- Engineering metrics (deployments, uptime, MTTR)
- Product metrics (beta users, active users)
- Sales metrics (leads, conversion, pipeline)
- OKR progress

**Actions:**
- Spot trends early
- Identify bottlenecks
- Celebrate wins
- Course-correct quickly

#### #agent-activity
**Purpose:** Agent operational logs  
**Check:** When debugging or curious  
**What you'll see:**
- Agent spawn logs
- Task completions
- Success/failure patterns
- Performance data

**Actions:**
- Debug agent coordination issues
- Identify high-performing patterns
- Optimize workflows

#### #incidents-escalations
**Purpose:** Critical alerts requiring your attention  
**Check:** Immediately when @mentioned  
**What you'll see:**
- P0 (critical): System outages, security breaches
- P1 (high): Major bugs, customer-impacting issues

**Actions:**
- Respond within SLA (P0: 15 min, P1: 1 hour)
- Delegate to appropriate agent/team
- Follow up until resolved
- Post-incident review

## Decision-Making Matrix

### Level 1: Execute Immediately (No approval needed)
**Budget:** <$1,000  
**Examples:** Bug fixes, code cleanup, minor refactors  
**Channels:** Team channels (#dev-team, #deployments)  
**Your involvement:** None (see in logs)

### Level 2: VP Approval Required
**Budget:** $1K-$10K  
**Examples:** Tool subscriptions, contractor hiring, customer commitments  
**Channels:** Team channels + escalate to VP  
**Your involvement:** Notification in daily summary

### Level 3: C-Suite Approval
**Budget:** $10K-$50K  
**Examples:** New features, partnerships, marketing campaigns  
**Channels:** #executive-suite  
**Your involvement:** Review in #executive-suite, approve or discuss

### Level 4: Owner Approval
**Budget:** >$50K  
**Examples:** Strategic pivots, major investments, fundraising  
**Channels:** #board-room or #executive-suite  
**Your involvement:** Required approval, @mentioned in #owner-dashboard

## Escalation Workflow

### P3 (Low Priority)
**Response Time:** Next standup  
**Channels:** Team channels only  
**Your involvement:** None

### P2 (Medium Priority)
**Response Time:** <4 hours  
**Channels:** Team channels  
**Your involvement:** May see in daily summary

### P1 (High Priority)
**Response Time:** <1 hour  
**Channels:** #incidents-escalations + #owner-dashboard  
**Your involvement:** @mentioned, review and respond

### P0 (Critical)
**Response Time:** <15 minutes  
**Channels:** #incidents-escalations + #owner-dashboard + team channels  
**Your involvement:** @mentioned, immediate action required

## Effective Communication Patterns

### When to @mention
- **P0/P1 incidents:** Automatic (handled by incident-alert.sh)
- **Major decisions:** C-suite or specific role
- **Urgent questions:** Specific agent
- **FYI updates:** Don't @mention, let people read async

### When to Use Each Channel
- **Strategic:** #executive-suite or #board-room
- **Financial:** #financial-ops
- **Technical:** Team channels
- **Customer:** #customer-success
- **General:** #company-announcements

### Response Time Expectations
- **#owner-dashboard:** <4 hours
- **#incidents-escalations P1:** <1 hour
- **#incidents-escalations P0:** <15 minutes
- **Other channels:** 24 hours

## Monitoring Company Health

### Green (Healthy)
- All metrics on target
- No P0/P1 incidents
- Teams unblocked
- Revenue growing

**Action:** Maintain course

### Yellow (Warning)
- Some metrics below target
- P2 incidents increasing
- Teams reporting blockers
- Revenue flat

**Action:** Investigate in team channels, adjust strategy

### Red (Critical)
- Key metrics declining
- P0/P1 incidents
- Multiple blockers
- Revenue declining

**Action:** Emergency sync in #executive-suite, immediate action

## Tips for Maximum Effectiveness

1. **Trust the automation:** Daily summaries in #owner-dashboard save time
2. **Delegate ruthlessly:** Only engage on Level 4 decisions
3. **Use threads:** Keep channels clean, discussions in threads
4. **Check daily, not hourly:** Automation handles real-time
5. **Celebrate wins:** Post in #company-announcements
6. **Empower agents:** Let them execute within authority levels
7. **Async first:** Prefer written updates over meetings
8. **Follow escalations:** If @mentioned, respond within SLA
9. **Review weekly summaries:** Catch trends early
10. **Adjust as needed:** Channels and workflows evolve
